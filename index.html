Neo-Versailles

Neo-Versailles was designed by capstone students at the ArtCenter of California. As an RIT student, I was part of a team brought on to assist with the programming on the project. Neo-Versailles is a First Person Puzzle Horror RPG where the player explores a destroyed palace of Versailles as well as corrupted paintings. The game uses Unity Engine. As a team, we utilized SCRUM methodologies, Confluence, Jira, Perforce, and Github after some problems occurred with our Perforce server.

During the first semester, the programming team focused on getting the base systems into the game. Personally, I worked on environmental scripting needed for levels, the backend of our inventory, the player health and ammo systems, and assisted other developers with debugging. The project has builds and code available upon request.

As of Spring semester, I am currently working on polishing code for the inventory systems and key items for the project. I am also experimenting with features for VFX and Materials to tie in the overall paint theming.
