Painted World: Neo-Versailles


Neo-Versailles was designed by capstone students at the ArtCenter of California. As an RIT student, I was part of a team brought on to assist with the programming for the project.

Neo-Versailles is a First-Person Puzzle RPG Horror where the player explores a destroyed palace of Versailles through corrupted paintings. The player utilizes paint to explore the world and defend themself from the Demon of Despair.

Skills:
C#, Unity Engine, Perforce, Jira, Confluence

The basis of the inventory code created by both myself and another RIT student, Megan Schier, is being utilized in this year's collaboration with ArtCenter.

Changeling


During Summer 2023 I worked on the entrepreneurial co-op project Changeling. Changeling is a VR game where the player explores mindscapes of members of a family to solve the mystery of what happened to the baby. At the time I joined the project, it had been in development for around 3 years.

My role as the lead technical artist on the project was to coordinate visual changes to the game in conjunction with the art team and to ensure that the game was ready for early access by the end of the year. That summer was the first time Changeling had dedicated technical artists and the game suffered from increasingly low frame rates, making some scenes nauseating to play through. The visuals had drastically different styles due to the majority of the assets being free or bought assets from various asset stores.

Skills:
Unreal Engine 4, Lighting & Material Optimization, Technical Documentation, Perforce
